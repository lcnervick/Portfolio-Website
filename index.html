What is Optometry?

Optometry is the medical field involving everything that has to do with your eyes and vision. This includes examination, diagnosis and treatment of the eyes and surrounding structures, and the treatment of vision problems. Common areas of practice include correction and/or treatment for low-vision, near-sightedness and far-sightedness, glaucoma, cataracts, retinal disorders, myopia, color-blindness and systemic diseases.

What is an Optometrist?

Doctors of Optometry (ODs) are the independent primary health care professionals for the eye. Optometrists examine, diagnose, treat, and manage diseases, injuries, and disorders of the visual system, the eye, and associated structures as well as identify related systemic conditions affecting the eye (American Optometric Association [AOA]).
Note: Optometrists should not be confused with ophthalmologists or dispensing opticians. An ophthalmologist is a physician who also performs eye surgery as well as diagnosing and treating eye conditions. Dispensing opticians fit eyeglasses and contact lenses, following prescriptions written by ophthalmologists or optometrists.

Optometry Services Offered

Visiting an optometrist on a regular basis is in everyone's best interest. Eye health is something that gets overlooked and neglected much too often. If left undiscovered or ignored, eye conditions can quickly become very serious and can affect the entire body's health.

It is highly recommended to undergo regular eye examinations as this is one of the main tools our optometrists use to helps our patients care for their eyesight. Keep reading to find out about the other services that we offer at our optometry clinic.

  • Comprehensive Eye Examinations

    Thorough eye examinations to assess visual acuity, refractive errors (such as nearsightedness, farsightedness, and astigmatism), eye coordination, and overall eye health. We may also evaluate the patient's medical history and discuss any concerns or symptoms.

  • Prescription of Corrective Lenses

    Prescribe and fit corrective lenses, such as eyeglasses or contact lenses, to address refractive errors and improve visual acuity. We will determine the appropriate lens power and type based on the individual's needs and preferences.

  • Contact Lens Fitting and Evaluation

    Fitting contact lenses for patients who prefer them over traditional eyeglasses. We will assess the patient's eye health, measure the curvature of the cornea, and recommend suitable contact lens options. We will also provide instructions on lens care and hygiene.

  • Diagnosis and Management of Eye Conditions

    Diagnose and manage various eye conditions, including but not limited to, cataracts, glaucoma, diabetic retinopathy, macular degeneration, dry eye syndrome, and conjunctivitis (pink eye). We may prescribe medications, provide treatment plans, or refer patients to ophthalmologists for specialized care if necessary.

  • Vision Therapy

    Vision therapy services for patients with certain visual disorders or conditions, such as amblyopia (lazy eye) or strabismus (crossed eyes). Vision therapy involves a series of exercises and techniques designed to improve visual function and coordination.

  • Pre- and Post-operative Care

    We work closely with ophthalmologists and provide pre- and post-operative care for patients undergoing eye surgeries, such as LASIK or cataract surgery. We monitor the patient's progress, manage any complications, and ensure optimal visual outcomes.

  • Pediatric Eye Care

    Eye care services for children. We conduct comprehensive eye exams to detect and manage vision problems, amblyopia, or other pediatric eye conditions. We may also collaborate with pediatricians and educators to address vision-related issues that can impact a child's learning and development.

Our Expert Optometry Staff

Dr. Kayla NervickOptometrist

Dr. Kayla Nervick, OD., graduated as part of the inaugural class of the Arizona College of Optometry at Midwestern University. Over the course of her career, she has gained much experience treating a wide variety of vision maladies and disease states in the very young to the elderly. As an extern working at the Indian Health Services Hospital in Parker, Arizona, Dr. Nervick worked with patients in all stages of life. That experience allowed her to acquire valuable experience with ocular diseases such as diabetic retinopathy, cataracts, glaucoma, keratoconus, severe dry eyes and more. Through the course of her career, she has found a great interest in the treatment of patients with irregular corneas due to keratoconus, corneal transplants and high amounts of astigmatism with specialty contact lenses. In all aspects of her practice, Dr. Nervick has always put her patients first and is always looking for ways to expand her knowledge in continued patient care practices.

Dr. Nervick specializes in several valuable modes of practice. Her experience with conventional soft and rigid gas permeable contact lenses allows her to fit a wide variety of patients into the right contact lenses with a high success rate. Additionally, an externship in a vision therapy practice and four years of working almost exclusively with children allowed her the chance to excel in well-child check-ups and assessments of children with various vision conditions. Most importantly, Dr. Nervick strives to take the extra steps to educate her patients about their eyes for treatment and preventative measures. Throughout her career, she has enjoyed participating in public health fairs, children’s vision screenings, and other community education efforts. On her time off, Dr. Nervick likes party planning, crafts, and spending time with her family.
